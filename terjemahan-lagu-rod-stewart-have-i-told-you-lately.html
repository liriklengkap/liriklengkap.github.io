3. "I have told you" or "I had told you"? Which is true? Explain!​


Both "I have told you" and "I had told you" can be true, but they are used in different contexts and convey different meanings.

"I have told you" is used in the present perfect tense. It implies that the action of telling you occurred in the past but has a connection or relevance to the present. This phrase suggests that the speaker has previously communicated the information to the listener and is emphasizing the current state of the situation.

10. : Have you been….…………… driving lesson lately? B: Yes, I have A take B takes C took D taking E taken


Jawaban:

B - takes

Penjelasan:

The sentence "Yes, I have" implies that the person is currently engaging in driving lessons or has done so in the recent past. Therefore, the correct verb tense to use in this context is present simple tense, which is "takes" in the third person singular form.

The options "take", "took", "taking", and "taken" are not correct because they are not in the present simple tense, or they do not agree with the subject "I".
